Sprint 3

In the third sprint, we successfully completed the electrical and firmware integration of the gas thruster reaction control system. This involved mapping out a diagram of connection placements on the proto board, soldering down electrical components to the proto board, and integrating the electrical system to the existing 4 thruster mechanical system.

The final mechanical milestone for sprint 3 included the design of an air tank holder, allowing both air tanks to be easily integrated with the rest of the mechanical system, while simultaneously staying as light as possible.

A few electrical issues came up during initial tests of the 4 thruster system. We witnessed a phenomenon where the solenoid valves would only open at lower tank pressures, which we hypothesized was due to blockage of the tubing system. Eventually, we figured out that the solenoid valves were not getting enough current to open at higher pressures. Luckily, the simple fix was to increase the current limit on the power supply!

Much of the software work for sprint 3 included tuning the 4 thruster PID loop and adding in a velocity component to achieve smoother balancing. Ultimately, by the end of sprint 3, we achieved our initial minimum viable product (MVP), as the drone was able to stabilize itself. If we were to continue work on the project, we'd want to make the drone more "flight ready". This would entail adding batteries as a portable power system, adding propellers as a lift mechanism, and including onboard pressure regulation to maximize airtime. All of us learned a lot in this project, and are happy with what we ended up creating!
