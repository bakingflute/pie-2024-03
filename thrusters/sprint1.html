Sprint 1

In the first sprint of our drone development project, our team focused on exploring the concept of a self-balancing drone. This process began with researching the topic, which involved watching instructional videos and reading about the control theory underlying such systems. With a stronger grasp of the concept, we proceeded to create initial sketches to visualize and refine the drone's design.

The next step involved planning the testing of the electronics and software while determining a way to start with a simple design and gradually build up to the final drone. Initially, we envisioned creating a pendulum setup with a single thruster. However, as we explored the complexities of achieving balance with just one thruster, we opted for a simpler approach: a seesaw mechanism with two thrusters positioned at opposite ends.

To integrate the thrusters into the system, we designed a circuit featuring an Arduino board that controlled two relays connected to solenoid valves. The valves were used to regulate the thrusters, enabling the seesaw to achieve balance. To detect the orientation of the seesaw, we utilized an Inertial Measurement Unit (MPU6050), which combines both an accelerometer and a gyroscope.

Finally, we developed code to process the values received from the IMU via the I2C interface and implemented a basic PID controller to balance the seesaw. The controller initially used only the angle data to determine which thruster to activate. The final PID implementation controlled the duration each thruster remained active to achieve precise balance.

As we move forward, our next steps involve transitioning from the current two-thruster seesaw design to a more complex four-thruster configuration. This development will build upon the insights and experiences gained during this sprint. Additionally, we plan to address an electrical issue where the Arduino board resets when the valves are toggled too quickly. This problem might be caused by flyback voltage or electromagnetic interference (EMF) noise generated by the valves or relays.
