Internal Components

Designing and adjusting the moving parts for this helmet was large component of making the helmet come to life. A double hinge system, where one set of hinges were driven by two servo motors and the other set guided the facemask along the appropriate arc, was used to create the quick actuation system for the facemask on the helmet. Since we wanted all potential users to be able to wear the helmet and experience all of the features, designing for accessibility was especially important. In order for anyone to be able to see the reflection of the OLED onto the acrylic eye pieces, we designed an adjustable OLED holder that could move vertically through a rack and pin system and rotate about an axis using a gear. Additionally, we made adjustable headbpieces that allowed the helmet to sit snug on any size head. Finally, a big goal for the project was for the entire system to be portable, so our CAD incorporated all of the mounting needed to house each electrical component inconspicuously as well as maintaining the practicality of each component.
