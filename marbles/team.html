Our Team

We are a group of five second year engineering students at Olin College of Engineering who worked together to create a marble sorting machine. Our team consists of two electrical and computer engineers, two mechanical engineers, and one systems engineer.

Toby Mallon is an electrical and computer engineer focused on integration and functionality between electrical and software components. He worked on proper wiring and voltage regulation on the electrical side and created a functional serial bridge between the image drawing interface and marble sorting firmware.

Arianne Fong is an electrical and computer engineer interested in using her technical skills alongside design thinking to create value for people. She focused on the software and electrical components for color sensing and marble sorting. Additionally, she helped with integration and developed the image drawing interface.

Jefferey Woodyard is a mechanical engineer passionate about combining hands-on design with technical problem-solving to create innovative solutions. For this project, he contributed to material selection and CAD modeling, developed the marble sorter and frame, and assisted in designing the marble basin. He also supported mechanical integration and manufacturing through advice, ensuring the project’s success through collaboration and precision.

Adiella Chapa is an aspiring mechanical engineer interested in design, optimization, and integration. For this project, she worked closely with Solidworks, laser cutters, and 3D printers. She prototyped various design ideas related to the marble basin structure and screw design and worked hard to create a functioning product. She worked on integrating the parts of her other members into the basin so that the product could run in a complete cycle. Further, she collaborated closely with her mechanical design team to get input on her design choices and give advice on her team’s choices.

Katherine Danielson is a sustainability and systems engineering student interested in systems design, integration, and project management–functioning as the project manager and a mechanical member. She worked closely with Solidworks and 3D-printers to design and fabricate the marble pathway and supports, aided in the lifting mechanism design, and marble frame, and helped anywhere else she was needed.
